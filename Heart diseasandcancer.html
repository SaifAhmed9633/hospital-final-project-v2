General surgery is a broad surgical specialty that focuses on the abdomen, gastrointestinal tract, and other areas of the body. General surgeons are trained to diagnose and treat a wide range of conditions, including: Abdominal problems, such as appendicitis, gallbladder disease, and hernias Breast cancer Colon and rectal cancer Endocrine disorders, such as thyroid disease Esophageal disorders Gastrointestinal bleeding Liver and pancreas diseases Skin cancer Soft tissue injuries Trauma General surgeons perform a variety of surgical procedures, including: Laparoscopic surgery, a minimally invasive technique that uses small incisions and a camera to perform surgery Open surgery, a more traditional surgical technique that involves larger incisions Robotic surgery, a minimally invasive technique that uses a robotic system to perform surgery General surgeons are also trained to provide preoperative and postoperative care. They work with a team of other healthcare professionals, such as anesthesiologists, nurses, and physician assistants, to provide comprehensive care for their patients.
